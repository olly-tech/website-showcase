Here is the Tips & Tricks page

Below, is a breakdown of the conjugation process.

  1. First identify the verb type:

    Regular Verbs:
    These follow predictable patterns based on their infinitive ending.

    • -er verbs: (e.g., parler - to speak)
    • -ir verbs: (e.g., abolir - to abolish)
    • -re verbs: (e.g., perdre - to lose)

    OR

    Irregular Verbs:
    These have unique conjugation patterns that must be memorised.

  2. Determine the Tense: Here we are doing present tense

    (French has various tenses, including present, past, future, and conditional. Each tense has its own set of endings that you'll need to add to the verb stem.)

  3. Find the Verb Stem:

    Remove the infinitive ending (-er, -ir, or -re) from the verb. For example, the stem of parler is parl-.

  4. Add the Correct Ending:

    The ending you add depends on the subject pronoun (je, tu, il/elle/on, nous, vous, ils/elles) and the tense.

  5. Practice and Memorize:

    Use flashcards, online resources, or language learning apps to practice verb conjugations. Focus on the most common verbs and tenses first.

  6. Immerse yourself in French by watching movies, listening to music, or reading books.

  7. Key Points to Remember:

    • Irregular verbs have unique conjugation patterns that must be memorized.
    • Some tenses are simple (conjugated with a single verb), while others are compound (using an auxiliary verb like avoir or être).
    • Pay attention to the subject pronoun when conjugating verbs.
    • Practice regularly to improve your fluency and accuracy.

Remember making mistakes shows that you are learning. Don't be afraid to make mistakes; it's part of the learning process.
