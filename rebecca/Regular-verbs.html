Regular Verbs

Regular verbs are rule-followers.
They woke up in the morning, looked out at the conflict-filled world, and chose peace.
Perhaps, they were thinking of us and wanted to bring us Zen and Peace and Good Vibes.

Regular verbs are rule followers. Once you learn those rules, you'll be able to conjugate any regular verb; even if, you have never seen it before in your life.

You may have noticed that French verbs all end in either: "er", "ir", or "re". There are different endings for each of these three types of regular verbs.
But, they all have one thing in common, when conjugating.
First cut off er/ir/re part of the verb and then add then ending.

Examples of regular verbs

French English
Punir To punish
Nourrir To nourish
Fondre To melt
Confondre To confuse
