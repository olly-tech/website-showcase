Aller

Conjugating "Aller" in the present tense

"Aller" means "to go" in French.

Pronoun Verb
je vais
tu vas
il/ elle/ on va
nous allons
vous allez
ils/ elles vont

"Aller" is an irregular verb but it is a very frquently used. So unfortunately, you have to just suck it up and learn it.

Conjugating "Aller" in the present tense is the first step to forming the immediate future tense in French so if you wish to communicate about your future plans, (perhaps, next year's holiday) you need to learn this.
