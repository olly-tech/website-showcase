Key words & Terminology


Here are the definitions for some keywords and some grammatical names of different classes of words that I use in my explanations.

Infinitve

The infinitive is the basic form of a verb, which is preceded by the word 'to'. It is a verb form that means 'to do something'
For example: to play, to dance, to say, to scream, to be, to have.
All of the above are examples of infinitives.

Conjugation

Conjugation is the change that takes place in a verb to express tense, mood, person and so on.
For example- when conjugating the verb 'to run', in the present tense- you conjugate it like this: I run, you run, he/she/it runs, we run, they run.
Conjugation is the noun. To conjugate is the infinitive form of the verb.

Regular Verbs

Regular verbs follow a predictable pattern (rule)- when being conjugated (changing tenses or changing the person).
In short, regular verbs are rule-followers or goody-two-shoes, who wouldn't even dream of breaking a rule.

Irregular verbs

Irregular verbs don't follow the normal and predictable pattern (rule)- when being conjugated (when changing tenses or when changing the pronoun).
In short irregular verbs are rule-breakers or chaotic little gremlins, who are completely unpredictable.

Stem

In French verb conjugation, the "stem" (also called the "radical") is the part of the verb that remains constant when conjugating, and it's formed by removing the infinitive ending (-er, -ir, or -re).
For example, the "stem" of the infinitive "habit-er" is "habit".
