Faire

Conjugating "Faire" in the present tense

"Faire" means "to do/make" in French.

Pronoun Verb
je fais
tu fais
il/ elle/ on fait
nous faisons
vous faites
ils/ elles font

"Faire" is an irregular verb but it is a very frquently used. So unfortunately, you have to just suck it up and learn it.

Conjugating "Faire" in the present tense is important to communicate about doing exercises, playing (non-ball) sports, doing homework, and making cake.
It also helps to communcate actions you did to another person or actions that affected you.

If you wish to have a normal conversation or navigate a francophone country; you'll need to have "Faire" down pat
